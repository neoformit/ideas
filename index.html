Good (and terrible) ideas for stuff

Academic data indexing and sharing service

  • Academics publish lots of data in wordy bulky papers
  • To find results you have to search papers, which is ineffective and slow
  • Solution: create a web app which indexes academic results/data in various ways
  • Link data to associated publications - authors get found more easily and increase citations
  • Could become a really attractive service for publishing houses and academics alike

Seafood sustainability

  • Fisheries data is available for download on gov websites
  • Make a site which has a page for each species, to show people what is most and least sustainable
  • Graph data such as habitat impact, bycatch rates and conservation status for biggest fisheries
  • How many dolphins/turtles killed per 100 fish? How much ground trawled over?
  • Can we get plotter data from fisheries?
  • Show a general page on which fishing methods are best, and why some fish are ok to eat!
  • Consider trying to dive and record trawled ground to show people what trawler damage actually looks like.
  • Try and get some plotting data from fisheries, aggregate data is supposedly publicly available.

Wax wraps...

  • ...are an awesome idea but are so expensive!
  • If you could sell them for $3-5 each (not $40!!) heaps more people would use them.
  • Surely they can be made cheaper by upcycling shirts etc - textile waste is a big problem.
  • Main bottlenecks would be a good supply of wax and efficiently cutting up textiles.
  • Should be even cheaper using non-beeswax wax - food-grade parafin wax is cheap, scalable and plant-based (Vegan!).
  • Could retail online and at market stalls - would scale easily into a large, potentially international business.
  • Are there H&S issues with using recycled fabric? Surely boiling it would be good enough.
  • They are food packaging, so do they have to meet some sort of food standard?

unharrass.org

  • Online resource of legal advice for harassment victims
  • Pro-bono legal service for victims
  • Sue companies, institutions and people for income
  • Shake-down organisations to reform their policies and procedures
